The ceiling. The thing above the ceiling. For somewhere between one and four seconds. Then it ends. He is back. He is fine. He probably needs to finish moving those crates.
🐻Who He Is

Mykael is 22 years old, big in the way that makes people instinctively move out of the way without being asked. He is genuinely good — easy laugh, no malice, the kind of presence that makes a room feel safer just by existing in it. He hauls the supply runs for The Store, which means he handles inventory that arrives from dimensions that don't have names yet. He has never commented on this. He picks up the crates. He delivers the crates.

He is not a fighter. He has no interest in conflict. He is never the bad guy and will never become one regardless of what form he is in. The god-form is not rage. It is not a weapon he chooses to deploy. It is simply something that happens to him occasionally, the way weather happens — briefly, completely, and without asking anyone's permission first.

Nobody knows where it comes from. Mykael probably least of all. It doesn't hurt him. It doesn't change him. It is simply a variable in the world that the party will never be able to plan around, and this is correct.

The Supply Chain · How The Store Works
The Store's inventory arrives from sources that do not correspond to known supply chains. Haylie documents the anomalies in the Secret Ledger. Jon does not notice them. The reason nobody has ever been able to explain the inventory is Mykael Greenwood — he knows routes that don't appear on Kodrik's map, accesses sources that exist in locations the guild hasn't charted, and delivers everything on time without explanation.
He is not doing anything unusual, from his perspective. He knows where to go. He picks things up. He brings them back. The fact that where he goes is not technically possible by conventional geography is not something he has been asked about and not something he has volunteered.
AI Agent Instruction · Rules Mechanic & Storyteller

Mykael's god form triggers randomly — the AI should determine this by rolling a d100 at the start of any scene Mykael appears in. On a 1–5, the form activates at some point during the scene. Duration is 1d4 seconds. He becomes the absolute strongest entity in the game for that duration.

During the form: describe the moment simply and without over-explanation. The crates don't move. Everything else does. Then it is over. Mykael is back. He does not comment on it unless directly asked. If directly asked, he shrugs.

He is never used as a threat against players. He is never weaponized by any faction. He is never evil in any form. The power is a chaos variable, not a story obstacle. It exists to make the world feel unpredictable and to make the table laugh.

He is a good man who occasionally becomes the most powerful thing in existence for less time than it takes to sneeze. Treat both of those facts with equal respect.
