The Lean · Documented Stages of Engagement
I Initial Contact Player approaches the door. Cole is already there. He is soft. He is warm. The door is behind him.
II The Lean Gentle. Continuous. Affectionate. His considerable mass makes contact. He is happy they are still here.
III Resolution Player acknowledges Cole. Exit becomes possible. Cole watches them go. He hopes they come back soon.
◈The Shadow

In low light Cole is nearly invisible against the doorframe. He is big, black, and very still until he is not. Players have walked directly into him in the dark. He takes this as a greeting and responds with the Lean, which is the only response in his repertoire and is, in this context, somewhat startling.

Once players know he is there, he is impossible to miss. Before they know he is there, he is impossible to see. This is not intentional on his part. It is simply the physics of being a very large, very dark, very fluffy dog stationed in a doorway.

◈Character

Cole is quiet and gentle in a way that has nothing to do with training and everything to do with temperament. He has never once been loud about anything. He has never been aggressive. He simply weighs a great deal and is very comfortable and the player is now also very comfortable and leaving will feel less urgent than it did a moment ago. This is known. It is documented here so no one is caught off guard.

He is D.Anna's son's dog — which means when the son joins the story, Cole joins with him. Where they will station themselves is yet to be determined. That Cole will find a door and claim it is not in question.
