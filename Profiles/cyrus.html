Tall. Lean-framed. A Great Dane's long-legged build draped in a Great Pyrenees coat the color of old smoke and new snow. He moves through the guildhall like weather — inevitable, unhurried, already there before you noticed him arrive. Nobody has confirmed what he is. Kodrik has never offered an explanation. The guild has collectively decided not to press the matter.
❄The Deterrent

Cyrus does not threaten. He does not posture. He does not growl as a warning. He raises his head from the hearthstone and the room goes quiet — not because of what he might do, but because of the space between what he might do and what anyone can verify he is capable of.

This is his entire function as deterrent, and it has never once failed.

❄Presence in the World

Cyrus is present in every Ironhold Keep scene without being announced. He is there as the fire is there, as the stone walls are there. Players will notice him on their own timeline. This is the correct way for it to happen.

He does not follow Kodrik through the castle. He is at the hearth. Kodrik returns to the hearth. This distinction matters to Cyrus, apparently.

❄Lineage

Cyrus and Keeva — D.Anna's Divine Hound — are mates. Their bloodlines produced Teddy, the Protector-in-Training currently at D.Anna's side. This means the two dominant powers of Ironhold Keep share a family through their animal companions. Nobody has remarked on the symbolism of this. It feels unwise to.

DM Notes · Handle With Care

Do not stat Cyrus. Do not roll for Cyrus. Do not give him initiative. His power is entirely atmospheric. A stat block reduces him to a number, and numbers can be managed. Cyrus cannot be managed. This is the point.

Include him in all Ironhold Keep scene descriptions without calling him out by name. Let the players notice him themselves. The moment a player asks "wait — is that a wolf?" is exactly the moment his profile should be working.
