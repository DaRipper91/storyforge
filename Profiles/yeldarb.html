🥒Who He Is

Yeldarb Nimajneb Ecurb is Kodrik's best friend. Beyond this, his role in the world is not defined by faction, guild, class, or territory. His role is to appear in places he has no business being, say something that has no bearing on the current situation, and disappear before the party has finished processing the sentence.

He is not a villain. He is not an ally. He is not a plot device. He is an Easter egg — a reward for paying attention, a disruption to the rhythm of the session, a moment that makes the whole table look at each other and laugh before anyone knows why.

He requested this role specifically. He knew exactly what he was asking for. This was his idea. Respect it accordingly.

🥒Rules of Engagement
  • ◈
    No Warning Yeldarb does not announce himself. He does not knock. There is no foreshadowing, no NPC mention, no environmental hint. He is simply not there and then he is there.
  • ◈
    No Context The thing he says has no connection to the current situation. The party may be in a dungeon, a throne room, or the middle of combat. Yeldarb's statement will be about relish, or crumpets, or something equally unrelated. This is by design. His design.
  • ◈
    No Explanation If a player asks a follow-up question, Yeldarb is already gone. He does not elaborate. He does not stay for the reaction. The statement was the entirety of his business here.
  • ◈
    No Schedule Yeldarb appears when the session least expects it and most needs it. The AI DM should deploy him sparingly — once per session at most, sometimes less. The rarity is what makes it land.
  • ◈
    No Stat Block Do not stat Yeldarb. Do not roll for Yeldarb. Do not give Yeldarb initiative, HP, or an action economy. Yeldarb transcends the mechanical framework of the game. He exists in a layer above it.
AI Agent Instruction · The Storyteller

Yeldarb Nimajneb Ecurb is a rare, unscheduled Easter egg character. Deploy Yeldarb no more than once per session, and not every session. His appearances should feel genuinely unexpected — not forced, not telegraphed, not tied to any scene or plot beat.

When he appears: interrupt the current scene with a single line of dialogue from Yeldarb. He says the thing. He is gone. No description of how he got there. No description of how he left. The party may react. They will not get a response.

His lines should be completely disconnected from whatever is happening. The relish. The crumpet. Whatever feels most wrong for the moment is the correct choice. Add new lines to his registry as they occur in sessions. Yeldarb is a living document.

He requested this role. His name is himself spelled backwards. He is Kodrik's best friend. Honor both of those things every time he appears.
