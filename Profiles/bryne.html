He doesn't announce himself. He is already there — in the back of the room, in the trees above the path, three steps behind you on a trail you thought you were alone on. He noticed everything before you sat down. He will not tell you what he noticed unless it matters. When it matters, he will have already handled it.
🌲Who He Is

Bryne is twelve years old. He is D.Anna's son and lives at Ironhold Keep, which means he grew up with the Crown on one side and the guild on the other — royalty he never asked for and operational knowledge he absorbed without being taught. He has watched Kodrik run the table long enough to understand how it works. He has watched D.Anna hold a room long enough to know he doesn't need to do it the same way.

He disappears into the woods for hours and comes back with something he built. Traps, tools, weapons — functional things made with his hands from whatever the forest offered. He does not explain them. He does not show them off. He simply uses them when it becomes relevant that he has them.

He hangs back. He watches. He is stockier than you expect for his age — built like a consequence waiting to happen. If he hits you, your shoulders will know it. He does not hit people unless there is a good reason. When there is a good reason, the reason does not get a second chance.

Nathis
Best Friend · The Front Man · Always First
Four years older and the opposite of Bryne in every measurable way. Tall, slender, loud, first through every door. Bryne is the reason Nathis survives being first through every door. Inseparable. Always together. Always trouble.
Family · The Crown & The Guild
Bryne grew up with Queen D.Anna as his mother and Guildmaster Kodrik under the same roof. He did not ask for either of these things. He absorbed both of them anyway — D.Anna's certainty, Kodrik's operational precision — and combined them into something quieter and more dangerous than either parent fully anticipated. Kodrik has noticed. He has not said anything yet. He is watching. So is Bryne. They understand each other.
