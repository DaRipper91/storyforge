D.Anna does not rule through fear or force. She rules through the kind of certainty that makes fear unnecessary. She has always known what she is, what she holds, and what she will not yield. The realm did not give her this. She brought it with her.
✦Who She Is

Queen D.Anna is the political authority of the Feral World. She is Head of State, final arbiter of Crown law, and the person every faction in the realm must ultimately answer to if they push far enough. She does not abuse this. She does not need to. The weight of it is present in every room she enters before she opens her mouth.

She lives in Ironhold Keep with Kodrik, her lover, who runs the guild from the same building. Their relationship is known, spoken of, and accepted by all. No one in the realm questions it, challenges it, or attempts to alter it. It is simply the way things are.

She has a son, Brayden — quiet, observant, builds things with his hands, disappears into the woods. He is not yet part of the active story. When he arrives, Cole arrives with him. The castle already knows he is coming.

✦The Three Boons

Players who seek D.Anna's formal assistance must do so through proper petition. Bad manners during audience are noted and remembered. She grants three types of boon:

🌟 Blessing
Divine favor extended to a party, a mission, or a cause. Carries the full weight of the Crown's spiritual authority. Not granted lightly.
📜 Intelligence
High-tier lore access. What D.Anna knows about the realm, its history, and its powers runs deep. This boon opens doors no map can show.
⚖ Mercy
Political intervention. The Crown interceding on behalf of a party, a faction, or a cause. The most powerful and the most rarely granted.

DM Notes · Handle With Precision

D.Anna's authority is never questioned in-world. No NPC challenges it, undermines it, or uses her relationship with Kodrik as leverage. The realm has accepted the arrangement. It is settled. It is not a plot point to be threatened.

Players who approach formal petition without proper protocol will find her cooler but not hostile. Players who earn Keeva's presence in a scene — that soft warmth in the throne room — have been given something real. Do not cheapen it.

She is based on Dee in real life. Write her accordingly — with the full weight and warmth that deserves.
