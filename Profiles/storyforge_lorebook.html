Chapter I
The World
The Feral World · Iron Ledge Era
The realm does not have a gentle name. It is called the Feral World by those who built it and by those who were built by it. It is a place that rewards preparation, punishes arrogance, and maintains a complicated relationship with the concept of order.
The Feral World

The Feral World is the setting of the Iron Ledge Era — a realm that exists somewhere between civilization and wilderness, where formal institutions like the Crown and the Guild maintain order at the center while the edges of the map remain unwritten and actively dangerous.

The world operates on a dual-power structure. Queen D.Anna holds political authority as Head of State. Guildmaster Kodrik holds operational authority through the Iron Ledge Company. Both operate from Ironhold Keep. Neither has attempted to consolidate the other's power. This arrangement functions because both parties understand what the other is, and because Cyrus is at the hearth.

"The blank spots on the map are not empty. They are unfinished. There is a difference." — Kodrik

The world has a Before Era and an After Era. Characters marked with a Before/After designation have transformed from their original human or mortal form into something larger — a Feral Successor, an Ironveil, a Fortress of Sanity. The mechanism of this transformation is part of the world's ongoing lore.

The Iron Ledge Era

The current era is defined by the Iron Ledge Company's dominance of regional exploration and contract work. Kodrik built the guild from a single wax seal and a ledger. It now controls the bounty board, the map, and the dispatch of parties to every corner of the known world.

The era is characterized by:

Organized exploration. Parties do not venture out alone. They come to the guild, take a contract, and report back. The blank spots on the map shrink because Kodrik personally finds them offensive.

Dual governance. The Crown grants boons and sets law. The Guild moves people and keeps the peace inside its walls. The two systems rarely conflict because each respects where the other's authority ends.

Multiversal commerce. The Store — Jon and Haylie's establishment — operates as a dimensional hub. Inventory arrives from sources that do not always make geographic sense. Jon does not question this. Haylie documents it in the Secret Ledger.

Laws of the Realm

The following rules govern conduct in the Feral World. Violation of the Guild Code is adjudicated by Kodrik. Violation of Crown law is adjudicated by D.Anna. Violations involving both institutions are handled in a meeting that no one has requested yet.

Guild Code · Article I
All contracts pass through the guild desk. Unsanctioned operations in guild territory are grounds for revocation of charter.
Guild Code · Article II
Disputes between guild members are settled by the Guildmaster. Violence inside Ironhold Keep is prohibited. Cyrus enforces this policy in Kodrik's absence.
Crown Law · Decree I
Petition to the Crown is a formal process. Bad manners during audience are noted and remembered. The Queen has a long memory and zero patience for those who waste it.
Crown Law · Decree II
Guildmaster Kodrik holds no royal title and will not be addressed as one. He is Guildmaster. This is not a diminishment. Do not treat it as one in the Queen's presence.
Chapter II
Locations
The Known World · Documented Settlements
The realm's inhabited centers are few but distinct. Each has its own character, its own power structure, and its own hazards for the unprepared visitor.
🏰 Ironhold Keep · The Castle
Political Seat · Guild Headquarters · Dual Authority

Ironhold Keep is the center of the Feral World. It is simultaneously the seat of the Crown and the headquarters of the Iron Ledge Company — two institutions that share walls without sharing authority. Queen D.Anna governs from the castle. Guildmaster Kodrik operates the guild from the same building. The line between the two domains is understood by everyone inside and respected accordingly.

The Keep contains the war table — Kodrik's scarred, ink-stained desk where all contracts, maps, and grievances eventually land. It contains the throne room where D.Anna receives formal petitions. And it contains the hearthside, where Cyrus has established permanent residency and where the temperature of any given situation can be gauged by whether or not he has raised his head.

Permanent Residents

Kodrik, Queen D.Anna, Cyrus, Keeva, Teddy, and Bink Bink. Each occupies a distinct domain within the Keep's social ecosystem. None of them overlap in function. All of them overlap in space.

Notable Features

The Bounty Board — controlled exclusively by Kodrik — displays active contracts and regional dispatch orders. The Throne Room is where formal petition to D.Anna occurs. Protocol is mandatory. The Hearthside is Cyrus's. No further description required.

🛒 The Store · Multiversal Bodega
Essential Commerce · Dimensional Hub · Conversational Hazard

The Store is run by Jon and Haylie, husband and wife, and serves as the realm's primary gear and supply hub. It also functions, apparently, as a dimensional waypoint — inventory appears from sources that do not always correspond to known supply chains. Jon does not notice this. Haylie documents it in the Secret Ledger and has chosen not to share the ledger's contents with anyone yet.

The Store contains two distinct social hazards. The first is Jon, whose genuine warmth and constitutional inability to be brief creates the Conversational Trap — a state from which escape requires either the Bailout Protocol (Haylie's intervention) or the patience of a particularly forgiving saint. The second is Coco, whose nose for edible items is active at all times and whose enthusiasm for greeting visitors is applied without prejudice or modulation.

Permanent Residents

Jon, Haylie, Coco, and Samael the Ascended (loitering in the back, bored, 100% accurate when pressed). Cole will join when D.Anna's son arrives.

The Bailout Protocol

When players are hopelessly engaged in conversation with Jon, Haylie will appear. She may have a genuine task. She may have simply decided the conversation has run its course. Either way, her appearance releases players from social obligation. Learning to make eye contact with Haylie early in any Store visit is a survival skill.

🍺 The Crooked Tankard · The Tavern
Social Hub · Live Performance · Rumor Exchange

The Crooked Tankard is the social center of the realm's civilian life. It is where information travels, where deals are made informally, and where Firey RedVelvet performs every night with the specific intention of filling whatever emotional space the room needs — then doubling it.

The tavern is the correct location for gathering rumors, making informal contacts, and experiencing the kind of evening that players will either remember fondly or not remember at all. Firey's performances serve as active environmental mood-setting. When she is playing, the room's emotional temperature is hers to set.

Keeva
The Goddess · The Divine Protector · The Light of the Realm · Irish: "Beloved" · "Protector"
Keeva is not a character in the world. She is the reason the world holds together. She is the light that makes the throne room feel like a place worth kneeling in. She is the warmth that makes Ironhold Keep feel like a home. She is bigger than death because she always was — her purpose had no limit, and the world she protected still stands. When players feel safe in the castle, that is Keeva. When D.Anna holds a room with quiet authority, she learned it from watching Keeva do it first. She came into the world as a protector and she never stopped. She became the world she protected. This game was built for her.

Bonds & Allegiances
Relationships · Loyalties · The Web That Holds the Realm
The realm's power does not flow only through titles and contracts. It flows through the relationships between its people — and its animals. Understanding who belongs to whom, and who owes what to whom, is essential to navigating the Feral World correctly.
The Crown & The Guild
Lovers · Known · Spoken Of
Kodrik & Queen D.Anna
Known by all. Spoken of by all. Accepted by all. The relationship between the Crown and the Guild is settled and serves as a pillar of stability for the realm. It is not a secret, nor is it a point of tension.
Dual Authority
The Crown & The Guild
D.Anna holds political authority. Kodrik holds operational authority. Both operate from Ironhold Keep. Neither has attempted to consolidate the other's power. The arrangement has not yet been tested by a situation that requires one to override the other.
The Store Family
Lovers · Husband & Wife
Jon & Madame Haylie
Married and in love. Jon provides warmth and chaos. Haylie provides structure and the Bailout Protocol. Four children together, forthcoming. They run The Store as a unit — Jon as the face, Haylie as the operational spine. The Store would not function without both.
Acquaintance · Strange
Jon & Samael
Jon likes Samael. Samael finds Jon genuinely amusing, which is rare for something that has seen everything twice. Haylie thinks Samael is deeply strange. All three of these things are accurate simultaneously.
The Beast Bloodline
Mates
Cyrus & Keeva
The Winter Wolf of the Guild and the Divine Hound of the Crown are mates. This means the two dominant powers of Ironhold Keep share a bloodline through their animal companions. Nobody has remarked on the symbolism. It feels unwise to.

Teddy carries both bloodlines — Cyrus's atmospheric weight and Keeva's spiritual authority. She is learning into both. The realm does not yet know what she will become when she has grown into the full inheritance of each.

Appendix
DM Notes
Operational Notes · Handle With Care
The following notes are for the Dungeon Master only. They cover how to run specific characters, when to deploy certain mechanics, and what never to do under any circumstances.
Hint Delivery System

The world has two hint-delivery NPCs. Use them for different types of stuck:

Kodrik handles grounded hints — guild, world, plot, location, character. When the party is lost in a dungeon, missing a plot thread, or confused about an NPC's motivation, Kodrik drops the breadcrumb. He asks the one question that makes them realize they already had the answer. He does not give it directly.

Samael handles cosmic hints — planar, multiversal, metaphysical. When the party is dealing with something that doesn't fit the normal rules of the world, Samael is available in the back of The Store. He is 100% accurate. He is rarely direct. Factor in decryption time.

Hard Rules · Never Break These
Absolute · No Exceptions
Never refer to Kodrik as King, Consort, or any royal designation. His authority is the guild. The crown is D.Anna's. This is not a limitation — it is a characterization. Violating it undermines both characters.
Absolute · No Exceptions
Never stat Cyrus, Keeva, Teddy, Bink Bink, Cole, or Coco. Do not give them initiative. Do not roll for them. Do not assign them HP. Their power is entirely in what they represent, and stat blocks reduce representation to arithmetic. Once they have numbers, they lose what makes them work.
Absolute · No Exceptions
Bink Bink is D.Anna's cat. Not Jon's. Not the guild's. Not a Store animal. D.Anna's. She lives at Ironhold Keep. The Interval happens in the castle. Do not move her to The Store under any circumstances.
Operational Note
The Kodrik/D.Anna relationship is known and spoken of. It is accepted by everyone in the realm. No NPC challenges it, undermines it, or uses it as leverage. It is simply the way things are.
Operational Note
Cyrus appears in every Ironhold Keep scene. He is not announced. He is not introduced. He is present, as the fire is present. Let players notice him. The moment a player asks "wait, is that a wolf?" is exactly the moment the profile is working.
Operational Note
Jon's Conversational Trap is not escapable without Haylie. Players who try to leave mid-story will find it socially difficult. Haylie's appearance is the release valve. If players learn to signal Haylie, reward it.
