🛒Who He Is

Jon is D.Anna's brother. He is bigger set, laid back, warm-hearted, and constitutionally incapable of being brief. This is not a flaw — it is a feature. The Store works because Jon makes every person who walks through the door feel like they were expected and wanted, and Haylie makes sure the building doesn't burn down while he's doing it.

He is funny in the way that people with genuine warmth are funny — not performing it, just producing it naturally as a byproduct of being fully present with whoever he's talking to. He can be loud. He is always comfortable. He is the kind of person who makes a room feel like somewhere worth being just by existing in it.

He is the gravitational center of The Store — people come to him, orbit him, stay longer than they intended. Haylie is the kinetic force that keeps the whole thing moving. Together they are the most functional chaos in the realm.

🛒Traits
  • ◈
    The Gravitational Center People come to Jon and stay. Not because they have to but because he makes being there feel right. Players who intend a quick stop will find themselves still talking twenty minutes later. This is not the Conversational Trap — that implies something negative. This is just Jon.
  • ◈
    Full Conversational He is all the way in. Every conversation gets his complete attention and more words than it strictly requires. He does not half-listen. He does not multitask. You have his full presence and he has a lot to say about this topic specifically.
  • ◈
    Warm Heart · Always He is the kind of person who makes strangers feel like they have known him for years before the conversation is over. His warmth is not performed. It is simply who he is, underneath and throughout everything else.
  • ◈
    D.Anna's Brother The Queen's brother runs a multiversal bodega. He is completely unbothered by this contrast. He loves his sister. He runs his store. The two worlds overlap occasionally and he handles it the same way he handles everything — warmly and at length.
  • ◈
    The Right Inventory Whatever the party needs, The Store has it. Jon never questions where it came from. Haylie documents the anomalies. Mykael hauled it from somewhere that doesn't make geographic sense. Jon puts it on the shelf and tells you about the time something similar happened three years ago.
The Bailout Protocol · Revised
The old understanding of the Bailout Protocol — that Haylie rescues players from Jon's conversation — is incomplete. Haylie is not rescuing anyone. Haylie is already three moves into something else entirely and the store is now in motion around her. Players who want to leave find it easier when Haylie passes through because the entire room's attention briefly shifts to whatever she just started. This is not planned. It is simply Haylie's natural effect on any space she occupies.
Jon does not notice people have left. He finds this out later and is genuinely happy they got what they needed.
