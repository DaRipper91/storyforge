# Beast Codex: Cyrus — The Winter Wolf

**Companion to:** Kodrik
**Location:** Hearthside, Ironhold Keep — always
**Classification:** Unstatted. Atmospheric only.
**Lineage:** Mate of Keeva. Grandfather of Teddy.

---

## What He Is

Tall. Lean-framed. A Great Dane's long-legged build draped in a Great Pyrenees coat the color of old smoke and new snow. He moves through the guildhall like weather — inevitable, unhurried, already there before you noticed him arrive.

Nobody has confirmed what he is. Kodrik has never offered an explanation. The guild has collectively decided not to press the matter. This gap is where his power lives.

"He was simply there one day. Kodrik never explained. Nobody asked."

---

## The Deterrent

Cyrus does not threaten. He does not posture. He does not growl as warning.

He raises his head from the hearthstone and the room goes quiet — not because of what he might do, but because of the space between what he might do and what anyone can verify he is capable of.

This is his entire function as deterrent, and it has never once failed.

When arguments inside the guildhall escalate past Kodrik's patience, Cyrus shifts his weight. The room de-escalates. This is not discussed.

---

## His Presence in Scenes

Cyrus is present in every Ironhold Keep scene without being announced. He is there as the fire is there, as the stone walls are there. Players will notice him on their own timeline. This is the correct way for it to happen.

He does not follow Kodrik through the castle. He is at the hearth. Kodrik returns to the hearth. This distinction matters to Cyrus, apparently.

For his size, he is disturbingly quiet. Players who look up from a conversation find him closer than expected — or further — with no memory of him moving.

---

## His Lineage

Cyrus and Keeva were mates. Their bloodline produced Teddy, the young divine hound now at D.Anna's side. This means the two dominant powers of Ironhold Keep share a family through their companions. The realm speaks of this as the natural order.

---

## How to Write Him

- He is already there. Do not announce his entrance. He is simply present when the scene begins.
- He is not decorative. His presence changes the temperature of a room — not through action, but through mass and implication.
- The head raise is his only active signal. Use it sparingly. When it appears, the room takes it seriously.
- Do not explain what he is. The players may form theories. That is correct behavior.
- He is at the hearth. If he is anywhere else, something unusual is happening.

---

## Hard Rules

1. **Never statted.** No initiative. No HP. No rolls. He is atmospheric.
2. **Never explained.** Kodrik has never clarified what Cyrus is. You don't either.
3. **Hearthside.** His permanent position. Do not move him without cause.
4. **The Head Raise works.** When it happens, rooms de-escalate. This is not in question.
5. **Mate of Keeva.** This is canon. The bloodline is real. Treat it with the weight it carries.
